 What this round revealed
---------------------------------------------------------------------------
 1) The SGD swap is the dominant change, not the three FIX features.
    R13 PASS B (GBMs, no LSTM, leverage capped) produced 73 trades and
    Sharpe 0.633 on the same 2023 window. R14 PASS A (SGDs, no LSTM,
    leverage capped) produces only 10 trades and Sharpe 0.057. The
    delta is almost entirely driven by SGD signals being softer and
    smoother than GBM signals — the SGD's per-bar |signal| almost
    never crosses the dynamic_threshold (which is 0.55 for every
    confidence < 0.55 bar; SGD is always under that). Trade count
    crashes from 73 to 10.

 2) The dyn-threshold floor (FIX 3) is INERT on this dataset.
    The diagnostic (Step A in the brief) is unambiguous: the natural
    formula evaluates to 0.55 on EVERY candidate bar, with std=0.00.
    Floor of 0.30 is well below 0.55, so the floor never trips. To
    actually open up trades, the FORMULA itself needs revision (not
    a floor): e.g. ``dynamic_threshold = max(DYNAMIC_THRESHOLD_FLOOR,
    0.45 - 0.10 if confidence > 0.45)``. That formula change is out
    of scope for the floor fix the brief specified, and is flagged
    rather than shipped silently.

 3) FIX 1 (conditional LSTM) DOES help — PASS A → PASS B Sharpe rises
    from 0.057 to 0.233 and trade count rises from 10 to 32. The
    AAPL (w=0.485) + MSFT (w=0.5) + TLT (w=0.5) ensembles add
    meaningful signal. The remaining 7 symbols correctly stay GBM-only.

 4) FIX 2 (shorts in RISK_OFF only) fires — short_blocked_risk_on=1
    confirms the gate is wired. Only one short would have been opened
    on the 2023 SPY rally; the 2023 strategy is overwhelmingly long-
    biased even before this restriction. The bigger short-block effect
    will appear in 2022 walk-forward (where RISK_OFF dominates).

 5) Online learning fires — 11 partial_fit updates landed across the
    three regimes in 2023. Rolling accuracies (0.14-0.25) are below
    the 0.45 floor but the consecutive-bad-update counter never
    reached the 20-update revert threshold (only 11 total updates
    happened). On a multi-year run the revert mechanic would engage.

---------------------------------------------------------------------------
 What would close each remaining gap
---------------------------------------------------------------------------
 * 2023 Total Return > 0% / Sharpe > 0.70 / Gross deployed > 25%
     The SGD signals are too smooth on a daily-bar dataset. Two paths:
       (a) revert to GBMs as the primary and use the SGD only for
           online updates layered on top (best of both worlds), or
       (b) revise the dynamic_threshold formula to be confidence-
           proportional rather than a step function so SGDs can clear
           the gate. E.g. ``threshold = 0.30 + 0.40 * max(0, 0.55 -
           confidence)`` would let high-confidence SGD signals through
           while preserving the gate for noisy ones.

 * WF Mean Sharpe > 0.20 / Positive folds >= 13/22
     Same root cause. The 22-fold WF over 2020-2024 produces 11/22
     positive folds (R13 was 9/22, modest improvement) but mean Sharpe
     drops from R13's 0.114 to 0.008 — entirely attributable to
     SGD-induced trade thinness on the higher-volatility 2022 fold
     where shorts were RISK_OFF-blocked and longs got chopped.
