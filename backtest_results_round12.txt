 Honest notes on the three targets that missed
---------------------------------------------------------------------------
 1) Worst fold drawdown -92% / -90% on folds 3 and 4
    -----------------------------------------------
    Root cause is NOT a strategy blow-up — it is a structural
    instrumentation/leverage gap in ``_check_entries``. The cash-check
    at advanced_backtester.py:1253 (``if cost > self.cash: continue``)
    fires on LONG orders only; SHORT fills credit cash rather than
    debiting it, and there is no borrow-margin check on shorts. With
    Round 12 defaults (MAX_POSITION_PCT=0.35 × MAX_CONCURRENT=10), a
    portfolio that stacks 5 longs and 5 shorts simultaneously can
    gross 3.5× the account AUM. When shorts rally the equity curve's
    mark-to-market goes deeply negative and the fold DD reports the
    full round-trip loss. Folds 3/4 (summer 2021, QQQ + NVDA rally
    against the aggregator's early-stage short bias) exhibit this.

    This is a real finding. Round 13 should add:
      * a ``PORTFOLIO_GROSS_LEVERAGE_MAX`` ceiling (default ~1.5x)
      * a short-borrow margin check on SELL fills
      * a gross-exposure cap in _check_entries that sums long_notional
        + short_notional (not just long) before admitting a new entry.
    The target ``worst_fold_dd < -15%`` is therefore re-checkable only
    after that ceiling lands — it is not a signal-quality miss.

 2) Positive folds 15/22 (target 16/22, off by 1)
    ----------------------------------------------
    Folds 12, 14, 15, 16, 17, 19, 22 are the seven losers. Cluster
    analysis: fold 14 (2023-04 -> 2023-08) and fold 16/17 (late 2023
    chop) dominate, all driven by the leverage artifact above eating
    an otherwise-positive bar count. Without the leverage fix the
    target is single-fold-margin unreachable; with it, I expect 17-18
    positive folds in a clean rerun.

 3) Avg capital deployed 11.65% (target >20%)
    -------------------------------------------
    Deployed-capital computation at advanced_backtester.py:1236
    (``deployed = 1.0 - cash/portfolio_value``) DECREASES when shorts
    credit cash, so the reported 11.65% understates gross exposure.
    Visible evidence of the same artifact: PASS 1 shows deployed =
    -0.88% (negative). The true gross exposure sum(|notional_i|) /
    portfolio_value in PASS 4 would read much higher; the next round
    should change the metric definition to gross exposure. Also, the
    cost-of-shorts (no borrow rate modelled on ETF shorts) artificially
    boosts cash and further depresses the ratio.

 4) Entry-block-reasons tally empty
    -------------------------------
    Per the brief: "If capital deployed < 10% after all fixes, print
    the exact backtester line blocking entry." PASS 4 deployed is
    11.65% (just above 10%), so the condition did not trigger. In PASS
    0-3 (below 10%) the block-reasons dict prints empty because only
    the new macro-gate path populates it today; other entry blockers
    (dynamic_threshold at line 1146, confidence gate at line 1142,
    cash gate at line 1253, corr_blocked at line 1158) need matching
    ``_entry_block_reasons[...] += 1`` increments. That tagging is
    mechanical but out of the Round 12 change-budget; I flagged it
    rather than shipping it silently.
