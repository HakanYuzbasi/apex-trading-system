   The targets are not hit with the defaults specified in the brief.
   This is an honest measurement, not a goal-seeked number. The three

   expected behavioural change:

   * Partial exits lift the **win rate** from 29% to 52% (PASS 2) by
     locking in the +1R and +2R milestones on every winner before
     letting the final tranche trail.
   * Correlation gating cuts **capital deployed** by ~70% (5.14% →
     1.08%) and max drawdown by ~37% (-17.4% → -10.9%). The brief
     asked for both tighter DD *and* higher deployment — these move
     in opposite directions on a 7-symbol mega-cap universe that is
     dominated by a single correlation factor. A higher CORR_THRESHOLD
     (e.g. 0.85) would let more concurrent positions in but at the
     cost of the DD improvement.
   * Kelly sizing is correctly cold-start-safe but therefore never
     activates inside a single-year backtest (30-sample gate).

 Tuning paths to reach the brief's targets (follow-up — not shipped here)
---------------------------------------------------------------------------
   1. Lower KELLY_MIN_SAMPLES from 30 to 10 for short backtests or seed
      the aggregator from a prior live trade log so the edge estimate
      is warm at t=0.
   2. Raise MAX_POSITION_PCT from 0.15 to 0.35 to let Kelly scale up
      once the edge estimate crosses the gate, improving deployment.
   3. Raise PARTIAL_EXIT_R_STOP_MULT from 2.5 to 3.5-4.0 so the initial
      ATR stop is not hit by routine mean-reversion noise, improving
      both PF and the worst-fold DD.
   4. Raise CORR_THRESHOLD from 0.70 to 0.85 (looser) on mega-cap
      equities where baseline correlation is ~0.65; the current
      threshold blocks legitimate diversification attempts.

   All four levers are env-overridable (APEX_KELLY_MIN_SAMPLES,
   APEX_MAX_POSITION_PCT, APEX_PARTIAL_EXIT_R_STOP_MULT,
   APEX_CORR_THRESHOLD) so follow-up rounds can sweep without code
   changes.
