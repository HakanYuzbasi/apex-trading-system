 What this round actually proved
---------------------------------------------------------------------------
 The dominant Round 12 finding — that mean WF Sharpe was 0.828 — was
 a leverage-artifact, not strategy edge. With Reg-T-style short
 margin (Round 13 FIX 1) and a 1.5× gross-exposure cap, the same
 universe and same signal stack produces:

   * Worst-fold DD bounded at -10.20% (R12 was -92.12%) — FIX 1 works.
   * Mean WF Sharpe 0.114 — that is the TRUE edge. The R12 number
     reflected a 3.5× leveraged book whose mark-to-market PnL
     amplified noise.
   * Compounded return drops from +23.86% to +2.00% — same reason.

 The 2023 single-year backtest (PASS A vs PASS B) shows the leverage
 cap is largely INERT in 2023 because gross deployed never reaches the
 cap (max 20.24% << 150%). The remaining -42% DD on PASS A/B is real
 strategy loss, not leverage. PASS A and PASS B differ only because
 short proceeds no longer credit cash in PASS B, which slightly
 changes intermediate cash balances and trade execution timing — but
 the trades themselves are nearly identical (73 trades, same
 symbols).

 LSTM ensemble (PASS C) ROI is negative on this universe at the
 0.6 weight specified by the brief because 6/10 LSTMs train below
 50% test accuracy. The ensemble drowns the GBM signal with mostly
 noise. The trade count collapses from 73 -> 22 (only AAPL/SPY pass
 the gate when the LSTM dominates), worsening diversification and
 producing -15.89% return. Lowering ``LSTM_ENSEMBLE_WEIGHT`` to
 ~0.2 or making it symbol-conditional (only ensemble symbols whose
 LSTM beat 0.55 test accuracy: AAPL, MSFT, TLT) is the natural
 follow-up.

 Per-block tagging (FIX 2) is now wired at every continue site in
 ``_check_entries``. The 2023 run shows ONE dominant gate in every
 pass: ``dynamic_threshold`` filters 1314-1466 candidate entries.
 The remaining gates (corr_blocked, corr_size_zeroed, cash_insufficient,
 leverage_cap, macro_regime, share_size_zero, signal_error,
 invalid_symbol, confidence_gate) all read 0 in this run — that is
 NOT a tagging bug; it confirms the upstream signal threshold
 already filters everything below the dynamic confidence floor before
 the structural gates can bite.

 The deployed-capital metric now exposes BOTH the legacy net view
 (``avg_capital_deployed``) AND the new gross view
 (``avg_gross_deployed``). PASS A's net 10.47% vs gross 19.71% shows
 the previous metric was indeed understating exposure by ~half.

 Targets that miss — what would close the gap
---------------------------------------------------------------------------
 1) WF Mean Sharpe 0.80 / Positive folds 17/22:
    Won't happen on this signal set. The honest mean Sharpe is 0.11.
    A real path to 0.80 needs:
      * higher-frequency signal (1h bars — blocked by yfinance data)
      * fundamentally better primary signals (current GBM test acc
        is 0.45-0.56 across regimes; LSTMs 0.40-0.60)
      * larger universe with genuine factor diversity (commodities,
        rates, FX cross-asset; current universe is 1 SPY + 6 mega-cap
        equities + 3 ETFs).

 2) 2023 return > +5% / Sharpe > 0.80:
    The 2023 mega-cap tape was a +25% year for SPY. A long-only or
    mostly-long allocation would have captured that. The current
    signal stack is bidirectional (trades both BUY and SELL) and
    the SELL trades into the 2023 rally generated the -10% drag.
    Restricting shorts to a ``RISK_OFF`` regime (which 2023 had only
    41.7% of) would close most of the gap; the mechanic is in place
    via ``HIGH_BETA_SYMBOLS`` block but only on longs not shorts.

 3) Gross deployed > 25%:
    With dynamic_threshold gating 1300+ candidates and only ~75 making
    it to a fill, the supply of qualifying entries is the bottleneck —
    not the leverage cap. Either lower the dynamic_threshold floor
    (currently 0.45-0.55) or improve the primary signal so more
    candidates clear it.
